Select Coursework

I have selected and linked eight projects I have done throughout my coursework that I am particularly proud of, and that I believe showcase my passions, interest, and the tenants of good librarianship.

For each project, I have matched it with the Program Learning Outcome (PLO) it best aligns with, as well as one or more ALA Core Competencies.

HUD-LOD Project Proposal: A Linked Dataset of Hudson's History

I created a proposal to create a new linked dataset to store data regarding the history of my hometown of Hudson, Ohio. The project involved the evaluation of the many different types of dataset schemas, and planning for the best way to organize and store data regarding a city’s history.

This project aligned with PLO 4 (evaluate systems and technologies relevant to a particular information content) and ALA Core Competency 3 (organization of recorded knowledge and information).

Lantex Theater informational website 

I researched the history of the Lantex Theater, a historic theater in Texas. The project involved compiling information from a multitude of sources. The outcome of the project was creating a comprehensive information website about the theater, as well as creating structured data records related to the theater. It serves as a central and comprehensive place to document the history of a local cultural institution.

This project aligned with PLO 5 (identify needs and connect individuals and communities with information that engages and empowers them) and ALA Core Competencies 2A (concepts and issues related to the lifecycle of recorded knowledge and information, from creation through various stages of use to disposition) and 3 (organization of recorded knowledge and information).

Agency and Association Report

I researched the history and goals of two international organizations: the Arab League Educational, Cultural and Scientific Organization (ALECSO), and the Association of Caribbean University, Research and Institutional Libraries. This project gave great insights into the types, work, and challenges of information agencies around the world.

This project aligned with PLO 3 (analyze and engage in the changing cultural, educational and social roles and responsibilities of librarians/information professionals and the environments they work in within the global society) and ALA Core Competency 1E (current types of library and closely related information agencies).

Country Profile: Librarianship in Iran

I wrote a paper on the librarianship environment in Iran. This includes library statistics on Iran’s different types of libraries, librarian education, professional organizations, and key factors that impact Iran’s libraries. 

This project aligned with PLO 3 (Analyze and engage in the changing cultural, educational and social roles and responsibilities of librarians/information professionals and the environments they work in within the global society) and ALA Core Competencies 1C (The history of libraries and librarianship), 1E (current types of library and closely related information agencies), and 1F (national and international social, public, information, economic, and cultural policies and trends of significance to the library and information profession).

ReminderX: Stakeholder Design Review

I created a workflow and design for a new theoretical mobile application to allow people to track tasks and remind themselves. This course and project taught the end-to-end design workflow, and how to optimize the experience for all types of users.

This project aligned with PLO 1 (apply the field's functional theories, principles, values, ethics and skills to everyday practice) and ALA Core Competency 4B (the application of information, communication, assistive, and related technology and tools consistent with professional ethics and prevailing service norms and applications).

Effective Programming in Library Cafés: Research Proposal

Library Cafés provide unique opportunities to attract and engage patrons. With this essay, I synthesized existing research about the use of library cafés in libraries and their effectiveness. I then constructed a research plan to study and produce qualitative and quantitative data about the effectiveness and impact of cafes on a library’s patrons and their use of library resources.

This project aligned with PLO 2 (critique and synthesize research and identify appropriate research methodologies to solve problems in the the field) and ALA Core Competencies 1I (the techniques used to analyze complex problems and create appropriate solutions), 5E (the principles and methods of advocacy used to reach specific audiences to promote and explain concepts and services), and 6B (the central research findings and research literature of the field).

Sample Bookstore Database

With this project, I designed a normalized database to store data for a theoretical bookstore. This project gave great insight into the challenges of effective and organized data storage.

This project aligned with PLO 4 (evaluate systems and technologies relevant to a particular information content) and ALA Core Competency 4 (technological knowledge and skills).

Young Library Patrons: Information Needs

With this project, I synthesized research on young library patrons, including their biggest challenges and needs. Young patron needs include information, but also other services, including consoling. This paper also includes some of my own recommendations on how best to serve young patrons as a result of my research. Libraries serve patrons of all ages, and keeping up with the needs of new generations is vital.

This project aligned with PLO 5 (identify needs and connect individuals and communities with information that engages and empowers them) and ALA Core Competencies 1I (the techniques used to analyze complex problems and create appropriate solutions), 5E (the principles and methods of advocacy used to reach specific audiences to promote and explain concepts and services), 6B (the central research findings and research literature of the field, and 8 (administration and management).
